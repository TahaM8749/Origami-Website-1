Interesting Facts about Camels

  • Camels have furry ears
  • Camels can move easily across sand because of their specially designed feet
  • When they find water, they will drink as much as possible and store the excess in their humps

Interesting Facts about Chameleons

  • Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder
  • Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chameleons
  • Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches

Interesting Facts about Pigeons

  • Pigeons have outstanding navigational skills
  • Both female and male pigeons share the responsibility of caring for and raising their young
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities

Interesting Facts about Bears

  • In many early civilizations, bears were seen as a symbol of power, strength and love
  • Bears care deeply about family members and will risk their lives to keep a cub or sibling out of danger
  • Bears are bowlegged, which gives them excellent grip and balance

Interesting Facts about Pandas

  • Pandas spend about 10-16 hours a day feeding, mainly on bamboo
  • Pandas are great at climbing trees and can swim well
  • Pandas are born furless, pink and blind (the black and white fur comes when they're about three weeks old)

Interesting Facts about Flying Cicadas

  • Most cicadas have red-orange eyes, but some have blue, white or grayish eyes
  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs
  • Female cicadas may be attracted to the sound of motors
